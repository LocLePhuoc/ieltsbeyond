Not every gaming session needs to be an adrenaline-fueled test of skill. Sometimes, especially on a rainy afternoon with a cup of tea in hand, you just want something gentle.

## Stardew Valley

The gold standard of cozy gaming. There's a reason this game has sold over 30 million copies. Plant crops, befriend villagers, explore mines at your own pace. The pixel art is charming, the music is perfect, and the gameplay loop is endlessly satisfying without ever being stressful.

What strikes me most about Stardew Valley is how it respects your time. There's no pressure to min-max. You can spend an entire in-game year just fishing and decorating your farm, and the game never makes you feel like you're doing it wrong.

## A Short Hike

This one takes about two hours to complete, and every minute is a joy. You play as a bird climbing a mountain on a small island. There's no combat, no fail states — just exploration, charming dialogue, and the simple pleasure of movement.

The moment you reach the summit and look out over the island is one of my favorite experiences in gaming. It earns that moment not through difficulty, but through the quiet accumulation of small, delightful discoveries along the way.

## Unpacking

A game about unpacking boxes after moving into a new home. That's it. That's the whole game. And somehow it tells one of the most affecting stories I've experienced, entirely through the objects a person owns and where they choose to place them.

Each level represents a different stage of life, and the items you unpack tell a story of growth, heartbreak, and ultimately, finding your place. It's storytelling through environmental design at its most elegant.

## The Common Thread

What makes a cozy game work isn't the absence of challenge — it's the presence of care. These games are crafted with attention to the small details: the sound of rain on a farmhouse roof, the way light filters through trees, the satisfaction of placing a book on just the right shelf. They remind us that games don't always need to be about winning. Sometimes they can just be about being.
